Emergency Medical Guide

Quick access to emergency procedures and first aid instructions

Emergency: Call 911

If you're experiencing a medical emergency, call emergency services immediately

Chest Pain

  • Have the person sit down and rest immediately
  • Call emergency services (911)
  • Loosen any tight clothing
  • If prescribed, assist with nitroglycerin medication
  • Monitor breathing and consciousness
Warning: Chest pain could indicate a heart attack. Immediate medical attention is crucial.

Stroke Symptoms

  • Remember FAST:
  • Face - Check for facial drooping
  • Arms - Test arm weakness
  • Speech - Listen for slurred speech
  • Time - Call 911 immediately
Warning: Time is critical in stroke treatment. Every minute counts.

Breathing Difficulties

  • Help person into comfortable position
  • Ensure access to fresh air
  • Loosen tight clothing
  • Assist with prescribed inhaler if available
  • Call 911 if symptoms are severe
Warning: Severe breathing problems require immediate medical attention.

Severe Bleeding

  • Apply direct pressure to wound
  • Use clean cloth or sterile bandage
  • Elevate injured area if possible
  • Keep pressure until help arrives
  • Call 911 for severe bleeding
Warning: Seek immediate medical care for uncontrolled bleeding.
